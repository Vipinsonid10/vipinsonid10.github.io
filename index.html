FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers about eligibility, process, outcomes, and global participation.

Who can apply for these internships?
Students, recent graduates, and early-career professionals can apply. Most tracks are beginner-friendly and mentor-guided, with role-based options for intermediate learners as well.
Are internships remote and accessible globally?
Yes. The programs are designed for global access with remote-friendly delivery. Mentoring sessions and support are coordinated to help learners across different time zones.
What is the duration and weekly commitment?
Current internship cohorts are structured as 4-week programs with guided weekly milestones. Most learners spend around 8-11 hours per week depending on the selected track.
Will I work on real projects?
Yes. Each track includes practical deliverables and at least 3 live project components, so you can build a portfolio that is useful for applications and interviews.
Do I get mentorship and feedback?
Absolutely. You receive mentor checkpoints, project feedback, and guidance on improving implementation quality, problem-solving approach, and presentation of outcomes.
Is a certificate provided after completion?
Yes. Learners who complete required milestones and project submissions receive a completion certificate. Verification support is also available through the website.
How does the selection and onboarding process work?
You apply through the form, receive guidance on track fit, and then proceed with onboarding steps for your chosen cohort. Communication is shared through official contact channels.
Can I get help choosing the right internship track?
Yes. Use the Course Selector and advisor support to pick the best track based on your goals, current skills, and target role.
